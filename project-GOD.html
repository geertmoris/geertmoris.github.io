Project GOD

What's in a name?

During one of my previous jobs, at a rental company of sound and light installations, I was responsible for the rigging (maintenance, repairs, technical inspection, etc.). One of the tasks was the regular greasing of the chains of the electric hoists (2000 pieces, every 4 months); a time-consuming and boring job. Therefore I decided to automate this.


The machine did have to meet a number of requirements:

  • 5 types of hoists
  • 5 types of flightcases
  • 3 types of chains, different lengths
  • 3 different chain speeds
  • greasing without removing chain from hoist
  • safe to use
  • if anything goes wrong, the machine has to stop
  • feedback regarding proces error
  • Machine must be able to be made compact for transport by small van to our other locations abroad.

Then the design started: Searching for existing techniques and parts, study of pneumatics (with which I was not familiar), all kinds of calculations, designing parts that had to be made to measure, etc. And finally drawing everything in 3D.

The next steps were to purchase and/or have parts made. Meanwhile, I made schematics of the electricity, pneumatics and program for the PLC control.


Finally, everything had to be assembled, adjusted and tested. Then the machine was officially put into operation. It turned out to be a successful undertaking that took a lot of work out of my hands. I was able to enjoy it myself for a few more years before I left the company.


👉 GOD: The movie! Sorry, only with Dutch subtitles.😞


Now, more then 10 years later, the machine still works and the company has even had a second one made.

This was a very challenging project that I thoroughly enjoyed.
