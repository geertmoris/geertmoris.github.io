Nature is the most fascinating thing there is. It always provides surprise, variety and mystery.

That's why it's so much fun to spend time in it, to study it and to learn from it.


Since a few years I am a member of the board of the local branch of Natuurpunt (Scherpenheuvel-Zichem), where I take on several tasks. We manage more than 600 hectares of nature in our municipality, and we are still expanding it.


🎦 A view from the watch tower


But my greatest passion is observing; especially birds, butterflies and dragonflies. So I always go out with binoculars, telescope, compact camera and determination guides.

I enter all my nature observations on site through the app 'ObsMapp'. This way I have a nice log of all my observations on the online platform waarnemingen.be (though it works worldwide, as observation.org). Here I not only find my own observations, but I can also search for photo's, species, areas, other observers, etc. It is also a great help in the management of our nature reserves. It's a great platform!

I especially love the automatic image recognition in the app. It allows you to quickly identify species and learn about them.
